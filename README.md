# The transcriptome effect of fluticasone propionate on pericyte neural reprogramming in ischemic stroke model through single-cell RNA sequencing analysis

## Authors
Ammi Paul, Sarah Dilmaghani, Dr. Yi Wu, William Johnston, Dr. Jing Wang 

## Abstract
During an ischemic stroke, a blood clot blocks blood flow, blocking the uptake of oxygen and glucose to the brain. Treatment for ischemic stroke relies on administration of therapeutics within a short timeframe, before long-lasting tissue damage and neuronal death takes hold. Pericytes are cells in the brain that wrap around capillaries and allow their contraction. These cells have been shown to have the ability to transform into neural stem cells (precursor to neurons) under ischemic conditions—low oxygen and glucose. Neurons lose energy stores quickly during ischemic stroke, which leads to their death, but pericytes can switch their energy producing mechanism to glycolysis which may allow their survival. To address the issue of neuronal regeneration/repair after ischemic stroke, drugs that promote this transformation (known as neural reprogramming) are being investigated. A known drug promoter of pericyte neural reprogramming is Compound C. This compound, however, is not FDA-approved, so we are looking for other drugs that can promote pericyte neural reprogramming. Previous work has shown fluticasone propionate to be promising through screening of drugs that promote glycolysis. Single-cell RNA sequencing is a technique to see what genes cells are expressing. By using this technique to compare what genes cells express when they are given fluticasone propionate or compound C, we hope to better understand pericyte neural reprogramming using fluticasone propionate, and hope to harness it in the future to repair the brain after a stroke.
